August 2025

The first time I saw you 👀

Remember the quarrel between two managers the very first time you entered the office? :)

You later told me that I said something that day which made you think, “Hmm… he is cool.” 😉

We talked for the first time while walking to the security room to collect your ID card… and of course I said, “Call me sir.”

And just like that… our banter began.

From helping you figure out how to order lunch, to flipping your ID card (which you sooo didn’t like), it all started there. ❤️

Spring

Siri, play Viva La Vida 🎵

Monsoon was at its peak in Mumbai… while life was just beginning for us.

Outside everything was rain and dark clouds but somewhere it felt like spring had quietly started.

Those post-lunch walks… standing silently near the entrance gate… watching the clouds… feeling the rain.

We spoke about everything and nothing.

You once asked me how I found my way in Mumbai… and I told you, “It grows on you. Give it some time… and it becomes beautiful.” ❤️

Days passed… and we started growing fond of each other.

The banter, the deep discussions, the silly jokes… everything slowly began to feel warm.

Going Out

Siri, play Steal My Girl 🎶

Remember the first time we went out? Khau Gali, Kandivali.

Just a couple of days before that, you had brought me a Mars my favourite chocolate because I helped you with something in the office. 🍫

Slowly we started planning more… going out more… and feeling more at peace around each other.

Teaching you local train markings… the apps to track trains… the chaos and jokes inside the bogies… the songs we used to sing.

And that tiny umbrella that saved us from getting drenched more times than we can count. ☔

Little by little we understood each other. Habits, quirks, personalities, everything.

Hard Work

Siri, play Kabhi Kabhi Aditi 🎧

Remember that first JSP JIRA? 😂

You were sooo confused and anxious. I used to send you song lyrics knowing you were stressed. ❤️

It was around Diwali when we had our first hug after Traditional Day.

No one could ever convince me to come dressed traditionally… but for you I bought that kurta and came to office.

Your mumma was ill and you were working from home on JSP. I remember telling you, “Just go. I’ll take care of this.”

From there to your first big Java story for the dashboard…

Writing queries… controllers… service layers… DAO design… debugging while I had fever… learning the Hibernate way of doing things. 💻

And when that first big commit of yours finally went in… I was soooo proud of you. ✨

Enters Love

Siri, play Hona Tha Pyaar ❤️

Then came the year-end party.

I even rescheduled my flight and added a layover in Delhi because you were sad that we wouldn’t be together on your first office party.

That night… we realised for the first time that we loved each other.

Those crazy dance moves.

Our first selfie.

The first time I dropped you home.

The next day you wanted to meet at Food Madness… and I completed your sentence even before you finished saying it. 😉

The copying of habits… finishing each other’s thoughts… matching likes and dislikes.

It happened so many times that eventually we stopped being surprised by it. ❤️

Confession

Siri, play Yellow 💛

I was in Kanpur attending friends’ weddings when I sent you that letter.

You were sitting in the office when you read it.

I remember telling you, “Go work, don’t get distracted.”

And you replied, “How can I not be distracted after reading this… it’s so beautiful.” ❤️

We started expressing ourselves more openly.

I remember sending you that voice note early in the morning while leaving for Varanasi.

You told me later you had wondered what you had done in life to deserve so much love.

“I love you” slowly became something we said freely… naturally… almost all the time.

Happiness

This part of my life is called happiness ✨

Those beach outings.

The flowers. The songs.

The hugs… the little pecks on the cheeks.

We used to talk the entire day.

You once told me, “I can never stop fighting with you. What joy!” 😄

IDC ka South Indian food?

December’s empty office… songs playing on Mooney Robot… pancakes and aloo parathas in office.

“I am hungry” was the first message we sent each other every morning while waiting for the other to reach office. 🍳

The massive framework upgrade JIRA and how insanely hard we worked together.

The tiny letters with blue flowers on them.

The pink and violet flowers you kept at your home for days after I used to leave them at your desk early in the morning in office. 🌸

The surprise from your amazing friends.

Those three days of exploring Mumbai.

The sleepless nights when you were ill and I kept checking on you.

The secret heart signs we used to make across the office. ❤️

Late night production issues… walking home while singing songs.

The Christmas lights that made me so happy.

The silent care of warm water, pen & paper, voice notes of me singing terribly. 😅

Conference room karaoke while Frenny asked, “Is this a café?”

Cold messaging soo many people on LinkedIn to get your brother a referral.

And a million billion other tiny moments that made you call me cutie, monkeyy, babe and whatnot.

Future

I’ll visit every god, don’t worry 🙏

By the time you are seeing this… maybe we have already celebrated Valentine’s the way I planned.

Maybe you liked the candle light concert.

Maybe you liked that fancy Powai dinner.

You probably didn’t like the ukulele performance on the beach though. 😄

But know this one thing. I would visit every god I know to make this work dear sunshine.

I would move mountains for you.

I have always said this and I shall say it again. Your dreams would always be mine too. What luxury is it to be loved like this!

March has always been special to me. Something beautiful has always reached me for the long term in March.

When you see this, I hope we are standing together. Unshaken. Polite. Choosing love, care, courage and everyone’s happiness… including ours.

I would be here with you forever. Through thick, thin and everything.

Epilogue

Siri, play AP Dhillon / Pal Pal / Dooron Dooron 🎶

Like each and every day of our lives, I will keep making this page more beautiful every passing month so that one day when we become old sitting on the porch of a house you bought to fulfill your dream, we can just flip it open and relive each of our beautiful memories together <3

“We’ll sail a boat of banter on an ocean of love.”

I always told you that.

There is plenty of love up here… now time for banter.

Someone once asked me, “What year did you graduate?”

And I said, “I PASSED OUT in 2019.” 😆
